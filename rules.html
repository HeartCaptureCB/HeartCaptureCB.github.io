Rules

About

HeartCapture!! Chorus Battle (HCCB) is an Otome game-themed chorus battle, with a storyline that follows the plot of a fictional Otome game set in a Japanese high school. Teams take the role of the main character in the game and are free to romance any of the seven (7) capture targets. They do this by creating entries that correspond to each of the capture targets' interests.

There are a total of three (3) rounds, each themed around different story events. Each round lasts 6 weeks, with 2 weeks of judging in between.

​HCCB is an elimination-based chorus battle, meaning that the lowest-scoring teams of each round will not be able to continue.

Bullying, harassment and/or discrimination towards any participants and/or our HCCB team will not be tolerated whatsoever. If anyone is found to be doing so, it will result in an automatic disqualification.

Please contact the HCCB Twitter if you need to report anyone of this behaviour.

Team Guidelines

Teams must have a minimum of two vocalists, but other than that there are no requirements to how many vocalists or non-singing staff members one's team can have. However, teams can only have up to 12 members. Vocalists who also take staff roles only take up one member slot.

Teams must elect a team leader as the main point of contact for the CB staff, as well as a backup leader in case the regular leader is unavailable.

Teams may be altered freely before round two takes place, but the HCCB staff must be notified each time. If you fail to comply/contact the HCCB staff there may be a small deduction to your final score.

You may only join one team. However, if your team is eliminated, you are allowed to join another team afterwards.

Anon groups are allowed, however, when registering you must write your teams' anon and real aliases. Only the HCCB staff will know your real aliases.

All team members must have an active social media account which is not private.

Song Guidelines

Entries must be at least 2:30 minutes long, and no longer than 6:30 minutes. Any work past the 6 and a half minute mark will not be judged.

​Songs can be in any language, including constructed languages, as long as an English translation is provided to the judges. Be aware that we may not be familiar with all languages, and that we are most familiar with English and Japanese.

Submission Guidelines

All entries must be submitted via a form that will be available a week from the deadline for the entry to be judged.

​Your submission form must include:

  • A link to your entry
  • A link to mix and acapella
  • A link to the art folder
  • A link to a document containing credits of any resources ​used (e.g. backgrounds, arrangements that weren't created by your team for this entry)
  • A link to your group's script
  • A link to your group's theme interpretation and a list of any additional challenges you completed, with proof (if applicable)
  • An alternative link to your entry on sites such as Google Drive, Dropbox or other video hosting sites such as Vimeo, in case we are unable to watch your entry due to copyright.

Plagiarism

Any instances of traced or copied art will result in the staff member in question being disqualified immediately, as well as an automatic score of zero (0) in the applicable categories.

We do not consider very similar or inspired videos to other CB entries plagiarism, but be aware that this may cause you to receive a lower score.
